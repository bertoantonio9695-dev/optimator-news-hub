7 Best Fintech ETFs to Buy Now

The fintech industry has experienced tremendous growth in recent years, driven by advancements in technology and increasing demand for digital financial services. As a result, fintech ETFs have become a popular investment option for those looking to tap into this booming sector. In this article, we will explore the 7 best fintech ETFs to buy now, featuring a mix of established players and innovative disruptors.

The fintech industry is diverse, encompassing a range of sub-sectors including payments, lending, blockchain, and digital banking. To cater to this diversity, we have selected a range of ETFs that track different indices and offer exposure to various aspects of the fintech market. Here are our top picks:

  • ARK Fintech Innovation ETF (ARKF): This ETF tracks the performance of the ARK Fintech Index, which comprises companies involved in the development and implementation of fintech innovations.
  • Global X Fintech ETF (FINX): This ETF tracks the performance of the Indxx Innovative Transaction & Process Index, which includes companies that facilitate innovative financial transactions and processes.
  • VanEck Vectors Fintech ETF (FTXN): This ETF tracks the performance of the MVIS Global Fintech Index, which comprises companies that derive at least 50% of their revenue from fintech-related activities.
  • First Trust Indxx Innovative Transaction & Process ETF (FTXR): This ETF tracks the performance of the Indxx Innovative Transaction & Process Index, which includes companies that facilitate innovative financial transactions and processes.
  • Tortoise Digital Payments Infrastructure Fund (TPAY): This ETF tracks the performance of the Tortoise Digital Payments Infrastructure Index, which comprises companies that facilitate digital payments and related infrastructure.
  • PureFunds ETFx FinTech (FINQ): This ETF tracks the performance of the EQM PureFunds Fintech Index, which comprises companies that derive at least 50% of their revenue from fintech-related activities.
  • Amplify Blockchain ETF (BLOK): This ETF tracks the performance of the Blooming Insights Blockchain Index, which comprises companies that derive at least 50% of their revenue from blockchain-related activities.

When investing in fintech ETFs, it's essential to consider the unique characteristics of each fund, including their underlying indices, expense ratios, and holdings. By diversifying your portfolio with a mix of established players and innovative disruptors, you can tap into the growth potential of the fintech industry while minimizing risk.

Conclusion

The fintech industry is poised for continued growth, driven by increasing demand for digital financial services and advancements in technology. By investing in the 7 best fintech ETFs to buy now, you can gain exposure to a range of innovative companies and benefit from the sector's growth potential. Remember to do your research, consider your investment goals and risk tolerance, and consult with a financial advisor before making any investment decisions.
